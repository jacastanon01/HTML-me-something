Who am I?

My name is Jacob Castanon. I am currently enrolled in LaunchCode to sharpen my coding skills. My goal in this course is to start a career in and learn more about web development. This is my first webpage I've created using HTML. In this page, I will detail my journey toward my decision to pursue interst in this career path.

    • McCain Auditorium

    Head Usher

    I grauduated high school in 2011 and decided to go to Kansas State University. While attending college, I got a job at the university auditorium as an usher. As an usher, I got to attend all types of shows and school musicals/ orchestras. I really enjoyed my time at K-State; However, I ended up dropping out after my sophomore year.

    Fogo de Chao

    Busser

    Upon coming back to Kansas City, I worked at Fogo de Chao bussing tables. Although that was a great place to work, I did not enjoy the restaurant enviroment. Every weekend would be packed and very stressful. I struggled to maintain their fast pace, but sometimes it would be worth it because after every shift, we would enjoy a free meal.

    1-800-Got-Junk?

    Crew Leader

    After realizing I was not fit for the restaurant industry, I decided to try my luck with manual labor. I applied for a job as a junkman. I learned not to judge anything before you try it because it was one the better job experiences and it taught me a lot about profressionalism and maintaining a good work ethic. I started out as a crew member and eventually moved up to crew leader which enabled me to gain leadership skills. As a crew leader, I was allowed to control the pace of my route and lead. The job was more than just a glorified trashman, it as much a sales position as it was general labor. After working there for a nearly a year, we had cutbacks and I was off to another position.

    Barnes & Noble

    Receiver/ Stocker

    I began working at Barnes and Noble in the receiving/ shipping department. Once I got more comfortable I began stocking in the front of the store. I enjoyed this job, it was very low-key.

    The Roasterie

    Bean Team Member/ Delivery Driver/ Shipping Associate/ Cold Brew Operations/ Misc.

    I started out at the roasterie as a member of the bean team, where I would go to events and give out free samples of coffee while engaging in conversation with potential customers. Eventually, I was given an opportunity to work full-time as a delivery driver, delivering coffee to various cafes, restaurants and businesses. As of now, I still work here. My job duites now vary from delivering coffee on my route to helping out in shipping/ receiving and, more recently, getting involved in making our cold brew and operating the canning line.

    Future and Beyond!

    I am interested in progressing my career into a new field. I've learned what I do not like in a job, and I feel a career in programming will provide an enviroment I enjoy. There are ample opportunities in technology and I've worked enough jobs to know what I do not want to do. I am grateful for the experiences I have accumilated, but looking forward I am hoping to get my foot in the door in something new. I know I do not like fast-pace stress-filled enviroments. Though sometimes programming can be stressful, I view it more as a puzzle that stresses you out and less of an angry-coworker-yelling-at-you-for-being-slow type of stress. My past jobs have only motivated me to begin something new, no matter how difficult the journey may be.

    As of now, I only have experience with Python, HTML and CSS. I began messing around with html and css about a year ago, but I could never focus enough to finish learning the basics. I found it difficult to find time to learn a new skill while working full-time job and I was trying to teach myself with little to no structure. I began reading about coding on codecademy and freecodecamp but whenever it came to actually applying my skills with hands-on practice, I did not know where to start. I am excited to learn more about programming and code more!
